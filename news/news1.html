Elon Musk Creates Artificial Intelligence Company to Compete with Owner of ChatGPT

Elon Musk aims to build generative Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems. The billionaire is expected to launch a new AI startup to compete directly with OpenAI, responsible for ChatGPT.

The businessman registered a new company called X.AI on March 9th, being listed as the sole director. Jared Birchall, a former Morgan Stanley banker who manages his fortune, is listed as secretary.

To launch this new project, Elon Musk would have purchased thousands of high-power GPU processors from the American technology company Nvidia. These cutting-edge graphics cards are essential to their goal of building AI systems capable of ingesting large amounts of content and producing human-like writing or realistic images, a technology similar to that of ChatGPT.

This movement contrasts with the opinion expressed by Musk in a recent open letter, also signed by other sector experts, in which he asked for a pause in the development of this technology “for security reasons”.

Musk is recruiting engineers from leading artificial intelligence laboratories to go head-to-head with OpenAI, a group he co-founded in 2015, leaving three years after disagreements with the board of directors.

Source: Economic Diary
