Past Tense

The Past Simple Tense

The Simple Past Tense is the form of the verb used to describe an action that happened before the present time and is no longer happening.

Affirmative

Subject + past simple

I bought chocolate. / I did buy chocolate.

The bus arrived at 5 o’clock. / The bus did arrive at 5 o'clock.

Negative

The subject is followed by did not.

Subject + did not + verb past simple

I did not bought chocolate.

The bus did not (didn't) arrive at 5 o’clock.

Interrogative

The subject is followed by past simple form of the verb.

Did + subject + past simple + object?

Did I bought chocolate?

Did the bus arrive at 5 o’clock?

Interrogative Negative

The subject is followed by not.

Did + subject not + verb first form + object

Did I not bought chocolate?

Did the bus not arrive at 5 o’clock?

The Past Progressive/Continuous Tense

The past progressive tense is used for an ongoing action in the past at some time without indicating any certainity about starting and finishing time.

Examples:

They were talking about games.

Barney was looking for the latest brochure.

Affirmative

The subject is followed by helping verb. (Subject + was/were + ing verb).

I/he/she/it + was + ing.

you/we/they + were + ing.

Examples:

They were studying english language.

He was watching television.

Negative

The auxuliar verb is followed by not. (Subject + was/were not + ing verb).

I/he/she/it was not + ing.

I/you/we/they were not + ing.

Examples:

He was not reading a book.

You were not acting.

They were not participating.

It was not going good.

Interrogative

The auxuliar verb is followed by the subject. (was/were subject + ing verb).

Was I/he/she/it + ing verb.

Were you/we/they + ing verb.

Examples:

Was he reading a book?

Were you acting?

Who was coming?

Interrogative negative

The auxuliar verb is followed by the subject + not. (Was/were subject + not + ing verb).

Was I/he/she/it + not + ing verb.

Were you/we/they + not + ing verb.

Examples:

Was he not reading a book?

Were you not acting?

Was it not raining?

Why were you not purchasing?

The Past Perfect Tense

The past perfect tense is used to describe actions that completed before a certain moment in past. Some time expression of the past time is used in his tense and adverb to time already, before, since, all are used. It is also used in complex sentence to express which of the two actions completed first in the past. The action completed first is expressed in past perfect tense and the action completed after that is expressed in simple past tense.

Examples:

I had seen lakes.

Sam had scored two goals.

David had worked alongside two of the world's finest scientists in the field of entomology.

Affirmative

The subject is followed by helping verb. (Subject had + past participle).

He/she/it had + past participle.

I/you/we/they had + past participle.

Examples:

I had finished my homework.

They had opened a new shop.

She had already sent the telegram.

Negative

The helping verb is followed by not. (Subject + have/has + not + past participle).

He/she/it has not + past participle.

I am not + ing.

I/You/we/they have not + past participle

Examples:

I have not finished my homework.

They have not opened a new shop.

She has not already sent the telegram.

Interrogative

The helping verb is followed by the subject. (Have/has + subject + past participle).

Has he/she/it + past participle.

Have I/you/we/they + past participle.

Examples:

Have I finished my homework?

Have they opened a new shop?

Has she already sent the telegram?

Interrogative negative

The subject is followed by not. (Have/has + subject + not + past participle).

Has he/she/it + not + past participle?

Have I/you/we/they + not + past participle?

Examples:

Have I not finished my homework?

Have they not opened a new shop?

Has she not already sent the telegram?

The Present Perfect Continuous Tense

The present perfect continuous tense is used for a continuous activity that began in the past and continues into the present or a continuous activity that began in the past but has now finished (usually very recently).

Examples:

I have been reading a book.

Julie has been relying on a pay rise to pay her student loan.

Affirmative

The subject is followed by helping verb + been. (Subject + have/has + been + ing verb).

He/she/it has + been + ing verb.

I/You/we/they have + been + ing verb.

Examples:

The farmers have been harvesting.

He has been reading since morning.

Negative

The helping verb is followed by not + been. (Subject + have/has + not + been + ing verb).

He/she/it has not + been + ing verb.

I/you/we/they have not + been + ing verb.

Examples:

The farmers have not been harvesting.

He has not been reading since morning.

She has not already sent the telegram.

Interrogative

The helping verb is followed by the subject. (Have/has + subject + been + ing verb).

Has he/she/it + past participle.

Have I/you/we/they + past participle.

Examples:

Have the farmers been harvesting?

Has he been reading since morning?

Interrogative negative

The subject is followed by not. (Have/has + subject + not + been + ing verb).

Has he/she/it + not + been + ing verb?

Have I/you/we/they + not + been + ing verb?

Examples:

Have the farmers not been harvesting?

Has he not been reading since morning?
