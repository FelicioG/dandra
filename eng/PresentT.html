Present Tense

The Present Simple Tense

The simple Present Tense expresses a general truth or a customary action.

Affirmative

Uncle Joe wears glasses.

The sun rises on the east.

Ducks love water.

Negative

I, we, you, they or any plural use of do not. He, she and it use of does not.

Subject + do not/does not + verb first form

They do not go.

She does not like me.

Interrogative

I, we, you, they or any plural use of do. He, she and it use of does.

Do/does + subject + verb first form + object

Do you help orphans?

Does she go the temple?

Why do you misbehave?

Why does she go?

Interrogative Negative

I, we, you, they or any plural use of do. He, she and it use of does.

Do/does + subject not + verb first form + object

Do you not play football?

Does she not return?

Where do they not visit?

Why does she not play?

The Present Progressive/Continuous Tense

The present progressive tense is used to talk about actions in the present, or things that are still going on or happening now.

Where do they not visit?

Why does she not play?

Affirmative

The subject is followed by helping verb. (Subject + is/am/are + ing verb).

He/she/it is + ing.

I am + ing.

You/we/they are + ing.

Examples:

He is reading a book.

You are acting.

I am going.

It is going good.

Negative

The auxuliar verb is followed by not. (Subject + is/am/are not + ing verb).

He/she/it is not + ing.

I am not + ing.

You/we/they are not + ing.

Examples:

He is not reading a book.

You are not acting.

I am not going.

It is not going good.

Interrogative

The auxuliar verb is followed by the subject. (Is/am/are subject + ing verb).

Is he/she/it + ing verb.

Am I + ing verb.

Are you/we/they + ing verb.

Examples:

Is he reading a book?

Are you acting?

Am I going?

Is it going good?

Interrogative negative

The auxuliar verb is followed by the subject + not. (Is/am/are subject + not + ing verb).

Is he/she/it + not + ing verb.

Am I + not + ing verb.

Are you/we/they + not + ing verb.

Examples:

Is he not reading a book?

Are you not acting?

Am I not going?

Is it not going good?

Why is it not going good?

Why are not purchasing?

The Present Perfect Tense

The present perfect tense is used to describe actions that began in the past. These actions affect or explain the present. The formal verbs have and has are used as helping/auxiliar verbs to form the present perfect tense.

Examples:

I have seen lakes.

Sam has scored two goals.

It has not rained for months.

Have you found your keys yet?

I've just finished my shower.

Affirmative

The subject is followed by helping verb. (Subject + have/has + past participle).

He/she/it has + past participle.

I/You/we/they have + past participle.

Examples:

I have finished my homework.

They have opened a new shop.

She has already sent the telegram.

Negative

The helping verb is followed by not. (Subject + have/has + not + past participle).

He/she/it has not + past participle.

I am not + ing.

I/You/we/they have not + past participle

Examples:

I have not finished my homework.

They have not opened a new shop.

She has not already sent the telegram.

Interrogative

The helping verb is followed by the subject. (Have/has + subject + past participle).

Has he/she/it + past participle.

Have I/you/we/they + past participle.

Examples:

Have I finished my homework?

Have they opened a new shop?

Has she already sent the telegram?

Interrogative negative

The subject is followed by not. (Have/has + subject + not + past participle).

Has he/she/it + not + past participle?

Have I/you/we/they + not + past participle?

Examples:

Have I not finished my homework?

Have they not opened a new shop?

Has she not already sent the telegram?

The Present Perfect Continuous Tense

The present perfect continuous tense is used for a continuous activity that began in the past and continues into the present or a continuous activity that began in the past but has now finished (usually very recently).

Examples:

I have been reading a book.

Julie has been relying on a pay rise to pay her student loan.

Affirmative

The subject is followed by helping verb + been. (Subject + have/has + been + ing verb).

He/she/it has + been + ing verb.

I/You/we/they have + been + ing verb.

Examples:

The farmers have been harvesting.

He has been reading since morning.

Negative

The helping verb is followed by not + been. (Subject + have/has + not + been + ing verb).

He/she/it has not + been + ing verb.

I/you/we/they have not + been + ing verb.

Examples:

The farmers have not been harvesting.

He has not been reading since morning.

She has not already sent the telegram.

Interrogative

The helping verb is followed by the subject. (Have/has + subject + been + ing verb).

Has he/she/it + past participle.

Have I/you/we/they + past participle.

Examples:

Have the farmers been harvesting?

Has he been reading since morning?

Interrogative negative

The subject is followed by not. (Have/has + subject + not + been + ing verb).

Has he/she/it + not + been + ing verb?

Have I/you/we/they + not + been + ing verb?

Examples:

Have the farmers not been harvesting?

Has he not been reading since morning?
